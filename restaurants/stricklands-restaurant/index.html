The story

Sixty-five years on the table.

Three generations of the Strickland family have cooked breakfast and lunch in this town. The biscuits are the same. The table-number system is the same. The hours are the same. Here is the long version.

  1. 1960

    Essie Strickland opens the doors on Broad Street.

    Ms. Essie Strickland starts the restaurant on Broad Street in downtown Athens. A small cafeteria-style room, a meat-and-three menu, and a family name over the door. The town is a different town. The biscuit recipe is already the biscuit recipe.

  2. 1975

    Paul Strickland, 36, buys the restaurant from his Aunt Essie.

    Second generation takes the line. Paul had been working in the kitchen for years; the handover is quiet. A couple of seasons later, the restaurant moves a few blocks over to East Broad Street, across from the UGA Arch. Students start showing up for breakfast before class.

  3. 1995

    The Atlanta Highway move.

    After two decades on East Broad, the restaurant relocates west to 4723 Atlanta Highway. More room, more parking, the same menu. Regulars follow. So do the cooks. So does the bell at the cash register.

  4. 1997

    The building is remodeled and expanded.

    The dining room is enlarged, a proper meeting room is added for church groups and Rotary lunches, and the catering side of the business settles into a real kitchen. The recipes do not change. The expansion is made to serve more of them.

  5. 2012

    Voted Best Breakfast in Georgia.

    Readers across the state put us at the top of the list. We do not print the award on the menu. We tape the clipping near the register and keep making biscuits.

    Year approximate · client to confirm
  6. 2020

    Takeout only. The regulars keep us open.

    Like everyone, we spent the year at the curb. A to-go window, a short menu, and a line of familiar faces that did not go away. We are here because they stayed. We do not forget it.

  7. 2025

    Third generation joins the line.

    The next Stricklands are on the clock. Breakfast prep starts at five. The oven is the same oven. The recipe is the recipe. Sixty-five years in, the line works the way the line has always worked.

    Inferred · client to confirm
  8. Today

    Same tables, same biscuits, same table-number system.

    Open Monday through Saturday. Breakfast starts at six. Lunch runs to two. On Sunday we close for family. The rest of the week, we are where we have always been.
