On the menu this week From the kitchen · Week of April 20

Short days of cool weather, a few pomegranates still holding on.

The last of the winter satsumas came in from a small grove outside Fort Valley this week, and the kitchen has been leaning into them — segments over little gem lettuces, zest folded into the ricotta, the rinds drying on a sheet tray above the pass for something we have not quite decided on yet. It is the kind of produce that rewards restraint. A plate with three good ingredients, dressed quietly, finished with salt.

The ground lamb arrives from Benji's each Thursday and goes almost immediately onto the hummus, with piquillo peppers and pomegranate molasses and a pinch of dried mint from last summer. It is a dish that has been on the menu, in one form or another, since we opened in 2007. We keep making it because we keep wanting to eat it.

The pork milanesa came back this week — pounded thin, breaded in panko, crisped hard in a pan and dressed with a fermented sweet pepper aioli we have been working on since fall. It goes with a napa cabbage salad, marinated onion, watermelon radish, peanuts. A Tuesday plate. The kind of food we think about when we think about the word Mediterranean — a word that does not belong to any one country and, on our menu, mostly means attention.

We will be running the kale salad, the hanger steak, the butternut pizzette, and the Portuguese custard tart straight through the end of the month. The flourless chocolate cake, with stracciatella gelato, will stay as long as the pecans hold out.

— The kitchen · Chef Burns Sullivan

Wine & cellar

A short list, chosen with the plates in mind.

Our cellar leans to the southern rim of the Mediterranean and to the small producers we have worked with for years. Spain, Portugal, the Languedoc, a handful of Georgia wines when the vintage calls for it. We pour by the glass, by the carafe, and by the bottle.

The wine list changes with the kitchen. A pour that worked with the tomato soup in February will make way for something brighter when the asparagus lands. Ask the room; we are happy to guide.
