About the place

A lunch counter that took.

Three owners, nearly fifty years, the same cornbread pans.

The building at 1119 Martin Luther King Jr. Parkway opened as a restaurant in 1977. Henry Hanley called it Hanley's Hickory Pit. He smoked goat in the back and got famous for it. If you ask around Athens long enough, somebody still remembers that goat.

Mr. Hanley sold the place in 1981 to Jerry Brown, who ran it a year. Ed DeAngelo took it over in 1982, hung up a new sign that read Plantation Buffet, and put the steam line where the pit used to be. That was the shift from barbecue joint to lunch buffet. The name stuck for forty-something years.

"We're not trying to be anything but what we are. Lunch. Every day. For forty years."

Keith Gray bought the restaurant in 1998 and has run it ever since. His daughters — Jessica and Lauren — are the reason the awning out front now also reads J & L Buffet. Same kitchen. Same recipes. Same regulars.

There is a crowd here that has been eating Wednesday pot roast since Clinton was in office. There is a UGA groundskeeper who orders the same plate every Tuesday — chicken & dumplings, field peas, cornbread, sweet tea — and has for eleven years. Nobody made that up; his name is on a plaque in the back hallway.

The place is plain on the outside. Cinder block, gravel lot, hand-painted sign. Inside there is a salad bar, a hot line, and a dessert station. The cobbler is made that morning. The cornbread is baked in cast-iron pans that are older than the owner. The sweet tea is sweet in the way Southern sweet tea is supposed to be sweet, which is to say you will feel it.

We are open Monday through Friday, ten-thirty to two. We rest on the weekends. We'll feed you.
