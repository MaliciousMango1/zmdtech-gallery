Our story

Fifty-plus years on the corner of Broad and Jackson.

Mr. Haircut has been part of downtown Athens for longer than most of its customers have been alive. Here's how we got here.

The beginning

Two brothers, one corner, a flat top.

In 1968, brothers Joe and Raymond Duncan opened a small barbershop at the corner of Broad Street and Jackson Street, a block off the UGA Arch. They called it the Flat Top Barber Shop — the haircut of the moment, and a promise about the work inside.

For twenty-six years, the Duncans cut hair for students, professors, shopkeepers, and downtown office workers. Generations came up in their chairs.

A timeline

How Mr. Haircut became Mr. Haircut.

A short history of the Broad Street chair.

  • 1968

    Flat Top Barber Shop opens

    Joe and Raymond Duncan open the shop on the corner of Broad and Jackson, a block from the UGA campus. Downtown Athens is about to get very busy.

  • 1994

    Pam Mullins takes over

    After twenty-six years, the Duncans sell the business to Pam Mullins. The name changes to Mr. Haircut — the chairs and the customers stay put.

  • Today

    An all-women barber crew

    Mr. Haircut is worked by an all-women team of barbers who've been part of downtown for decades. Walk in on a weekday morning and you'll hear them catching up with regulars by name.

What makes it work

A family atmosphere that hasn't changed.

We pride ourselves on our customer service and family atmosphere. Some customers have been coming in for eighteen years — they know the barbers, the barbers know their hair, and nobody has to explain themselves twice.

We cut hair for everybody — professionals, students, kids, first-timers, and Athens locals who've been in this chair since before the Arch got its railings.

No appointments. No online booking system. Just walk in, grab a seat, and wait your turn.
