Our story

From a semester abroad to a downtown institution.

Community was never supposed to exist. It started with a German study-abroad student falling for the Athens music scene, then learning to sew, then selling vintage at flea markets. Fifteen years later we're one of the last independent boutiques left downtown.

The founder

Sanni Baumgärtner — founder, seamstress, boss.

Sanni came to Athens for a study-abroad program at UGA and ended up building a life here. For a decade, she played music. Somewhere in between, she started collecting vintage clothing, learning to sew, and selling reworked pieces at local markets.

In 2010, she launched her own clothing line — Community Service — using sustainable and vintage materials. That same year, she opened the shop to sell it alongside other Georgia makers she admired.

Today she still designs, sews, teaches classes, and runs the studio from the back of the store. If you stop in, there's a good chance you'll see her at the machine.

A timeline

How Community became Community.

The long version of a short story.

  • 2000s

    A semester abroad becomes a decade

    Sanni arrives in Athens on an exchange from Germany. She falls for the music scene, starts a band, and keeps extending the visa.

  • Late 2000s

    Flea market beginnings

    She begins collecting and reselling vintage clothing, teaching herself to sew, and experimenting with reconstructions sold at local markets.

  • 2010

    Community Service launches — and the shop opens

    The in-house clothing label launches. A few months later, Community opens on N Jackson St — a boutique for the new line plus other Georgia makers she wants to support.

  • 2013

    Flagpole names Community an Athens Favorite

    The local paper's reader awards put the shop on the map. Community has been named an Athens Favorite every year since.

  • 2019

    Sewing for the Dawgs begins

    UGA Athletics reaches out to ask if we can mend game-day jerseys. We say yes. Now in our sixth season.

  • Today

    50+ makers, a full studio, and sewing classes

    The shop carries over 50 Georgia-based designers and artists. The in-house studio runs alterations, custom design, and four-to-six week beginner-through-advanced sewing courses.

"Many of the problems in the textile industry developed through the disconnect between the people who make the clothes and the people who wear them."
— Community's founding philosophy
Why we do it this way

Sustainability, transparency, connection.

Three ideas we keep coming back to.

i.

Sustainability

Fast fashion is one of the largest polluting industries on the planet. Our response is modest — redesign vintage, use sustainable materials, and make clothes that last long enough to be worn out instead of thrown out.

ii.

Transparency

Our sewing studio is visible from the sales floor. You can see the machines, watch the work, ask who made the jacket you're trying on. The textile industry hides its labor — we don't.

iii.

Connection

We teach sewing classes because we think knowing how to mend, construct, and alter your own clothes is a skill worth preserving. A dozen or so students graduate every semester.
