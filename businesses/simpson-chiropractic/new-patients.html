New Patients

Start here.

First visits are mostly a conversation. Before you come in, here's what to bring, what to expect, and how to book.

Before your visit

What to bring.

For your first appointment

  • Photo ID and your insurance card, if you plan to use insurance.
  • A list of current medications and supplements.
  • Any recent imaging, lab work, or specialist notes related to your concern.
  • Your completed intake form (available below or at the front desk).
  • Comfortable clothing — something you can move in.

A few things to think about

  • When did the issue start? Has it changed over time?
  • What makes it better or worse in a typical day?
  • What have you already tried — and what felt like it helped?
  • What would "better" look like for you three months from now?
  • Any questions you want answered before starting care.
Your first visit

What to expect when you arrive.

  1. 01

    Check-in and paperwork

    Plan to arrive about fifteen minutes early. If you haven't filled out intake forms yet, we'll give you a clipboard and a quiet seat.

  2. 02

    Consultation with the clinician

    A conversation — history, goals, current symptoms, lifestyle. This is where most of the useful information comes out, so there's no rush.

  3. 03

    Examination

    A hands-on exam based on what you described. We may recommend imaging or additional evaluation if it's helpful — never as a default.

  4. 04

    Findings and a plan

    We walk you through what we found and what we recommend, in plain language. You'll know what care looks like, how long it should take, and what it costs before you commit.

  5. 05

    First treatment (if appropriate)

    If it makes sense for your case and you're ready, we can begin care the same day. If not, we'll schedule what's next.
